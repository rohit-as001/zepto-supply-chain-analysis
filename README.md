## Project Overview
ZeptoSupplyChain is a two-view interactive Excel dashboard built on Zepto's supply chain and sales data. It examines performance from two critical angles — an Overview of brand sales, category performance, daily demand trends, delivery partner rankings, and location-wise order volumes, and a Key Insights view that synthesizes brand intelligence, top product rankings, category health, and strategic observations into a decision-ready narrative.
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of the supply chain ecosystem by combining key operational metrics such as sales revenue, order volume, stock availability, returns, product performance, delivery partner efficiency, and regional demand patterns.

The objective of this project is to help management identify high-performing products and brands, optimize inventory levels, improve delivery efficiency, reduce returns, and enhance customer satisfaction through data-driven decision-making.

Together, these views give Zepto's leadership a complete operational picture — from which SKUs are flying off the shelf to which categories are underperforming, and from which delivery partners are most reliable to which city zones are driving the highest order density.

This project is designed for supply chain managers, category heads, demand planners, city operations leads, and e-commerce analytics teams operating in the fast-moving quick-commerce space.
Built using Power BI/Excel, the dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor supply chain performance in real time and uncover actionable insights that improve operational efficiency and profitability.

# Key Insights
1) Brand Performance — Dabur Leads, But Tata and Nestle Lag
    - **Dabur (₹356.2K) and Patanjali (₹351.0K)** are nearly tied at the top — both Indian heritage brands with strong mass-market recall — suggesting Zepto's customer base skews toward value-conscious, everyday essential buyers.
    - **Tata (₹168K) and Nestle (₹169K)** are the weakest performers despite being among India's most recognized consumer brands — a counter-intuitive result that warrants investigation into SKU selection, pricing, placement, or catalog depth for these brands.
    - **The ₹188K gap between Dabur and Tata** on a platform of only 10,000 orders is a material performance difference that cannot be attributed to chance.
      
    - **Actionable:** Zepto should review the Tata and Nestle product catalog on the platform — are the right SKUs listed? Are they priced competitively? Are they appearing in search results and promotional slots?


2) Top Products — Patanjali Ghee Dominates, Diverse Mix in Top 10
    - **Patanjali Ghee 1L at ₹351K** is the single highest-selling product — by a significant margin. This is a high-weight, high-value daily essential with strong repeat purchase behavior.
    - The top 10 products span across **grocery, personal care, health, and dairy** — indicating a genuinely multi-category basket rather than single-category dominance.
    - **Whisper Ultra Pads (₹212K)** ranking in the top 5 highlights strong demand for personal care essentials — a category where fast delivery has a distinct value proposition over traditional retail.
    - **Actionable:** The top 10 products should be placed on **perpetual stock guarantee** (zero stockout tolerance) and receive priority slot allocation in dark stores closest to the highest-order locations.


3) Product Category Performance — Grocery Dominates, Electronics and Fresh Lag
    - **Grocery (₹1.5M), Personal Care (₹1.3M), and Beverages (₹1.0M)** together account for the overwhelming majority of revenue — confirming Zepto's core value proposition lies in everyday essentials, not lifestyle or specialty categories.
    - **Electronics, Fruits, Vegetables, and Health** are severely underperforming relative to their potential category size in Indian quick-commerce.
    - Fresh categories (Fruits and Vegetables) underperforming is particularly notable — Zepto's platform promise includes fresh produce, yet it's among the weakest revenue contributors.
    - **Actionable:** For Fruits and Vegetables, the issue may be **supply chain freshness, pricing, or customer trust** in ordering perishables for 10-minute delivery. A targeted freshness guarantee campaign + competitive pricing audit could revive these categories. For Electronics, a curated "daily essentials electronics" sub-catalog (cables, batteries, bulbs) may perform better than general electronics.


4) Daily Sales Trend — Thursday Peak, Weekend Dip
    - Sales trend across the week: **Sunday → gradual rise → Wednesday/Thursday peak at ₹0.7M+ → Friday → Saturday dip.**
    - **Thursday is the peak sales day,** with the highest order concentration of the week.
    - **Saturday and Sunday show slight performance dips —** counter to what one might expect from a consumer app where weekends typically drive leisure shopping.
    - The weekend dip may reflect that Zepto's current customer base skews toward **working professionals and households** who plan grocery runs mid-week.
    - **Actionable:** Run **Thursday promotional pushes** (flash sales, category discounts) to maximize revenue on the already-peak day. For weekends, design **leisure and entertainment-focused promotions** (snacks, beverages, personal care bundles) to convert the weekend behavior pattern from dip to opportunity.


5) Delivery Partners — Karan Mehta Leads, but Top 5 Are Tightly Clustered (HIgh Performance or Maximum Delivery)
    - The **range between the top (1,054) and bottom (1,004) delivery partner is only 50** deliveries across the dataset — indicating an extremely even workload distribution.
    - This suggests either effective **auto-assignment algorithms** are balancing orders, or the workforce is similarly sized in capacity.
    - With delivery partner performance this close, the differentiator between partners shifts from volume to **quality metrics** — on-time delivery rate, customer ratings, return handling accuracy.
    - **Actionable:** Introduce a **delivery partner quality scorecard** combining deliveries completed, on-time %, customer rating average, and return handling accuracy. Volume alone doesn't reflect partner value in a 10-minute delivery model.


6) Location Performance — Jaipur Leads, Multi-City Presence is Strength
    - **Malviya Nagar, Jaipur** leading all locations with 1,622 orders is a stand-out finding — Jaipur is a Tier-2 city outperforming multiple metro locations including Mumbai and Bangalore zones.
    - This validates Zepto's **Tier-2 city expansion thesis** — quick-commerce demand is not limited to metro cities, and Jaipur may represent an underserved market with high organic adoption.
    - The **tight range across all locations (1,490–1,622)** suggests fairly even demand distribution — no single zone is dramatically over or under performing, indicating strong geographic diversification.
    - **Actionable:** Malviya Nagar, Jaipur deserves dedicated analysis — what's driving its order density? Is it a specific demographic, product mix, promotional history, or dark store proximity? Replicating the Jaipur success formula in similar Tier-2 cities (Lucknow, Indore, Bhopal) could unlock significant growth.


7) Stock vs. Sales — Healthy Inventory but Watch for Overstocking
    - Stock quantity stands at **2.68 Million units** against 29,820 units sold — implying a **stock-to-sales ratio of approximately 90:1.**
    - While maintaining safety stock is critical in quick-commerce (where a 10-minute promise requires ready inventory), a 90:1 ratio suggests **potential overstocking** in certain categories or SKUs.
    - Combined with **1,000 total returns (10% return rate),** there is a working capital efficiency question — is return inventory being restocked effectively, or is it being written off?
    - **Actionable:** Conduct an ABC analysis of stock — identify the top 20% of SKUs driving 80% of sales (likely the top 10 products identified above) and ensure these are never out of stock, while reducing safety stock levels for slow-moving SKUs in Electronics, Fruits, and Vegetables.




# Business Recommendations (Recommendations for Management)
  - **Inventory Optimization:** Balance stock levels to avoid overstocking in low-performing categories.
  - **Category Development:** Invest in underperforming categories (Electronics, Vegetables) with targeted promotions.
  - **Bundle Promotions:** Encourage larger basket sizes through combo deals and subscription packs.
  - **Delivery Partner Incentives:** Reward top-performing partners and train lower performers to improve consistency.
  - **Regional Strategy:** Prioritize stocking and marketing in Jaipur, Mumbai, Pune, and Bangalore.
  - **Customer Engagement:** Use loyalty programs and personalized offers to increase repeat purchases.


1) Priority 1 — Investigate and Fix Tata & Nestle Underperformance
    - Two of India's most powerful FMCG brands generating the lowest revenue on the platform is a significant missed opportunity. Management should audit the Tata and Nestle product listings — SKU selection relevance, pricing vs. competition, search visibility, and promotional history. A targeted joint business plan (JBP) with both brands to identify the right hero SKUs for quick-commerce can potentially unlock ₹150–200K in incremental revenue.

2) Priority 2 — Implement Zero-Stockout Policy for Top 10 SKUs
    - Patanjali Ghee 1L (₹351K), Horlicks Classic Malt (₹231K), and Head & Shoulders Shampoo (₹219K) are the revenue anchors of this platform. A single stockout event on any of these products during peak demand (Wednesday–Thursday) directly loses revenue, damages customer trust, and risks app abandonment. These SKUs must have dedicated safety stock buffers in every operational dark store.

3) Priority 3 — Revive Fresh Category Performance with a Trust Campaign
    - Fruits and Vegetables are chronically underperforming for a platform built on the promise of fresh grocery delivery. The barrier is likely customer trust in ordering perishables for 10-minute delivery. A "Freshness Guaranteed or Refunded" campaign, combined with origin story marketing (farm-to-door messaging) and competitive pricing on staple items (tomatoes, onions, bananas), could convert skeptical customers into repeat fresh buyers.

4) Priority 4 — Design a Thursday Peak Maximization Strategy
    - Thursday is the highest-sales day of the week. This is a predictable, recurring commercial opportunity. Management should implement a "Thursday Power Hours" campaign — a mid-week flash sale window (12 PM–3 PM) featuring 10–15% discounts on top-selling categories (Grocery, Personal Care, Beverages), combined with free delivery threshold reduction to drive basket size. The goal: push Thursday from ₹0.7M to ₹1.0M.          

5) Priority 5 — Build a Weekend Demand Generation Program
    - Saturday and Sunday showing dips in a consumer app context is an addressable problem, not an inherent pattern. Weekend shoppers have different intent — they're not doing functional grocery runs, they're in leisure mode. Design weekend-specific bundles: snack + beverage combos, party essentials packs, self-care Sunday kits (personal care bundles). Pair with weekend-exclusive offers to shift behavioral patterns over 4–6 weeks.

6) Priority 6 — Replicate the Jaipur Playbook in Tier-2 Cities
    - Malviya Nagar, Jaipur generating 1,622 orders — the highest of any location — deserves forensic analysis. What drove this adoption? Understand the Jaipur demographic, product mix, dark store location, and marketing history, then systematically apply the winning formula to untapped Tier-2 markets like Lucknow, Nagpur, Surat, and Indore. Quick-commerce adoption in Tier-2 cities is still early-stage — first-mover dominance there is the strategic prize.

7) Priority 7 — Introduce Delivery Partner Quality Scorecards
    - All five delivery partners handle nearly identical volumes — differentiation must come from quality, not quantity. Introduce monthly scorecards tracking: on-time delivery rate, customer satisfaction score, order accuracy rate, return handling compliance, and peak-hour availability. Use these scores to implement a tiered incentive structure that rewards quality performance, not just delivery count. This protects Zepto's 10-minute promise at the execution layer.
